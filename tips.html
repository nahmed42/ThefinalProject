Tips & Tricks

-Always have backup lesson plans. And maybe backup for the backups.
-Plan field trips at the beginning of the school year. The earlier the better.
-Be flexible. When plans don’t workout, and most times they won’t, just move on to the next one. Remember, your first year is all trial and error.
-Connect with parents! This is an important one. Doing this saved me a lot of unnecessary stress. I felt supported and connected to the parents all year, and when problems occurred, I knew that the parents will have my back, which was a great feeling.
-Send an email during the first week of school introducing yourself and add a little more information about your experience/hobbies. This will make the parents feel like they know you :)
-First WEEK of school is all about reinforcing rules and boundaries. This will make or break your year.
-You learn a lot from other educators. Connect with other teachers in your school.
-Stay out of gossip groups. You will not want extra stress or to be involved in drama with adults.
