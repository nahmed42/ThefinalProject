My Personal Experience

My initial year as an educator was quite the rollercoaster ride. Amidst countless moments of tears and laughter, I gained invaluable lessons. No matter how well-prepared one might be, encountering mistakes and unlearning old notions is an inevitable part of the learning process. Working at a small private school, where I was the sole grade teacher, presented so many challenges as I lacked the support system I needed. Nevertheless, the experience taught me a multitude of things that will undoubtedly stay with me throughout my entire career.
My main takeaways from my first year were invaluable. One crucial lesson I learned was the importance of taking as many personal days as possible. Being a teacher is an incredibly demanding job, requiring immense emotional and mental strength.
It became clear that I couldn't effectively teach or be present for my students if I neglected my well-being! Which, most of us do when we are first year teachers. As educators, we have a direct impact on our students' lives, making it essential to prioritize self-care and put ourselves first when needed. Only by taking care of yourself can you then truly thrive and provide the best support and guidance to those you are teaching. And believe me, they can feel and notice it.
